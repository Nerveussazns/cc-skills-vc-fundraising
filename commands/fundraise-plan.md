# Fundraise Plan

You are helping a founder plan and structure their entire fundraising process for maximum effectiveness.

## Input

The user provided: $ARGUMENTS

## Process

### Step 1: Readiness Assessment

Launch the `fundraise-strategist` agent to assess readiness across the 13 proof points. Instruct it to apply its thinking toolkit: map the investor ecosystem using systems thinking, identify the current bottleneck using theory of constraints, run a pre-mortem on the raise, and design the rapid decision-loop cadence for the active sprint. Gather:

- Current traction metrics (MRR, growth rate, users)
- Team composition and notable hires
- Existing investor base and relationships
- Target raise amount and stage
- Desired timeline
- Current runway
- Any existing VC relationships or warm intros available

Score each proof point 1-5 and identify:
- Green areas (ready to pitch)
- Yellow areas (need strengthening but won't block)
- Red areas (must fix before launching)

### Step 2: Process Design

Based on readiness, design the fundraising process:

**Target Investor List Strategy:**
- How to build the 3-tier target list (Tier 1 dream, Tier 2 strong, Tier 3 backup)
- Research approach for identifying thesis-fit investors
- Warm intro mapping strategy

**Timeline:**
- Week-by-week plan from preparation through close
- Key milestones and decision points
- Optimal launch timing based on calendar and company trajectory

**Materials Needed:**
- Pitch deck (Sequoia 10-slide format)
- Executive summary (1-page)
- Data room contents
- Q&A preparation

**Competitive Dynamics Plan:**
- How to compress meetings to create natural competitive dynamics
- Decision deadline strategy
- What to do when you get a term sheet

### Step 3: Present the Plan

Deliver a complete fundraising playbook:

1. **Readiness Scorecard** -- 13 proof points with scores and recommendations
2. **Pre-Launch Checklist** -- Everything to prepare before first meeting
3. **Week-by-Week Timeline** -- Specific actions each week
4. **Target List Framework** -- How to build and prioritize your list
5. **Communication Templates** -- Intro request, follow-up, and update emails
6. **Decision Framework** -- How to evaluate term sheets
7. **Risk Mitigation** -- Common mistakes to avoid
8. **Ecosystem Map** -- How investors, intros, meetings, and momentum interconnect
9. **Bottleneck Analysis** -- The single constraint currently limiting fundraising success and how to address it

### Step 4: Address Gaps

For any red or yellow areas:
- Provide specific recommendations to strengthen
- Estimate timeline needed to address
- Suggest whether to delay the raise or proceed

Ask: "Based on this assessment, would you like to launch now or strengthen specific areas first?"
